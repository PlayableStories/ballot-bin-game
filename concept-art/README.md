## ⚠️ Do not reuse these boards uncorrected

All three drifted from the brief in ways that matter. Anyone taking art direction from them must know:

**1. The control caption is wrong on every board.** They say *"TILT LEFT OR RIGHT TO FIGHT THE WIND"* / *"TILT TO COUNTER THE WIND"* / *"ANGLE AGAINST THE WIND"*. There is **no tilt control**. The game is swipe-angle only. Correct copy: **SWIPE UP TO THROW**, and on first run only, **ANGLE YOUR SWIPE TO BEAT THE WIND**.

**2. Boards A and C depict real politicians.** Board A is unmistakably Trump and Clinton; board C's right-hand figure is Trump-coded. **The game uses fictional archetypes only** — no real likenesses, names, or invented quotations. The prototype cast is THE STRONG LEADER and THE OUTSIDER. This is a hard line (`../CONCEPT.md` §6).

**3. Board A promises "LONGER THROW = MORE POINTS".** There are no points. The score is ballots in the bin, and the bin is at a fixed distance, so there is no long throw to reward.

**4. Every board stages red-left versus blue-right in mirror symmetry.** This hard-codes the wind as an ideological seesaw and hands the player the wrong reading — *"both sides push you around, they're all the same"* — which is a lazier point than the one the game makes. The shipping palette is **amber and teal**, and the two candidates may push the wind the *same* direction on consecutive speeches (`../CONCEPT.md` §12).

---

## What board C is actually good for

Composition and depth staging: the vanishing point on the bin, the throwing-lane floor arrow, podiums clear of the lane, the ball's dotted arc and its shadow, the HUD split of `IN THE BIN` / `ON THE FLOOR` / `TIME LEFT`. Take the layout. Leave the politicians, the palette, and the copy.
